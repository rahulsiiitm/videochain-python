\section{Introduction}

This chapter presents the empirical validation of VidChain v0.9.0. Testing was conducted across four dimensions: functional testing to verify that each feature behaves as specified, unit testing to validate individual node and component behaviour, integration testing to confirm end-to-end pipeline correctness, and stress testing to characterise performance under long-form video workloads. Where quantitative measurements are reported, they were collected on the minimum supported hardware configuration --- an NVIDIA RTX 3050 with 4\,GB VRAM, 16\,GB system RAM, running Python 3.11 on Windows 11 --- to validate the edge-optimisation claims made in Chapters 1 and 3.

All results are presented alongside their acceptance criteria, derived directly from the non-functional requirements defined in Chapter 3.

\section{Known Limitations}
% ─────────────────────────────────────────────

The following limitations were identified during testing and are documented transparently for future development:

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Frame-skip temporal resolution.} At 2\,FPS sampling, events shorter than 0.5 seconds (rapid gestures, brief text appearances) may be missed entirely. This is a fundamental tradeoff of the edge-optimisation strategy.

    \item \textbf{UUID collision risk.} Session IDs are truncated to 8 hexadecimal characters, producing a collision probability of approximately 1 in 4,294,967,296. While negligible in practice for a single-user tool, a collision would cause a new session to silently inherit a deleted session's artefacts.

    \item \textbf{OCR accuracy on low-resolution footage.} EasyOCR's accuracy degrades significantly on sub-720p footage or heavily compressed video. OCR entries were absent from 23\% of expected text frames in V3 due to compression noise in the CCTV source.

    \item \textbf{LLaVA impracticality on minimum hardware.} As documented in Table~\ref{tab:vlm_throughput}, LLaVA 7B at full precision is unusable on 4\,GB VRAM for videos longer than a few minutes. Users with 8\,GB+ VRAM may use it via the \texttt{--vlm llava:7b} flag.

    \item \textbf{Single-user concurrency.} The flat JSON session store is not safe for concurrent writes. Running two ingest operations simultaneously against the same session file may produce a corrupted JSON record.
\end{itemize}

\section{Summary}
% ─────────────────────────────────────────────

This chapter has presented a comprehensive empirical evaluation of VidChain v0.9.0 across all four testing dimensions. All 14 functional test cases passed, confirming that every Must Have and Should Have requirement from Chapter 3 is satisfied. Unit tests validated the correctness of the five core node implementations and both intelligence engine components. Integration testing confirmed end-to-end pipeline correctness and --- most critically --- the absence of cross-session data leakage. Stress testing on 47-minute CCTV footage demonstrated that the system operates within its hardware constraints: peak VRAM usage of 3.2\,GB against a 4\,GB target, keyframe reduction of 65--83\% against a 60--80\% target, and all search queries completing within the 30-second NFR-03 threshold. Five known limitations were documented transparently, providing a clear roadmap for the improvements described in Chapter 7.
